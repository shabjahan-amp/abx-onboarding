🕰 ABX at Amplitude — Context

Why ABX Matters at Amplitude ▼

Enterprise ABX at Amplitude is built on the insight that enterprise deals don't happen to you — they're orchestrated. In the enterprise motion, a single deal often involves multiple stakeholders, long sales cycles, and high deal values. Spray-and-pray marketing doesn't work here. ABX replaces volume with precision: picking the right accounts, activating the right programs, and aligning every motion behind shared account goals.

The ABX function was designed to be the connective tissue between marketing (reach, content, programs), sales (AE relationships, deal strategy), SDRs (outbound coordination), and leadership (executive engagement).

How ABX Differs from Field Marketing and Demand Gen ▼

ABX

  • Account-specific, named-account programs
  • AE-aligned and deal-stage aware
  • Buying-group focus (not just personas)
  • Tied to pipeline and revenue KPIs
  • Orchestrates across all channels for a specific account

Field Marketing / Demand Gen

  • Regional or segment programs
  • Audience and persona-based
  • Volume-focused (leads, MQLs)
  • Tied to pipeline creation and event metrics
  • Broad reach, one-to-many

Note: ABX partners with Field Marketing — we co-design programs, align targeting, and share account data. We're not competing channels.

🔍 How Accounts Are Selected

ABX strategy is aligned to "moonshot" opportunities

The goal is to focus efforts on high-impact, high-potential accounts where coordinated action can influence pipeline and revenue. FY26 reduced the account list from 142 to 118 to maximize impact through deeper personalization and tighter execution.

📈 Prospect Score

High or Very High Prospect Score based on firmographic fit, intent signals, and engagement history.

🎯 Buying Stage

Decision or Purchase buying stage in 6sense — accounts actively in-market for analytics or adjacent products.

💰 ARR Potential

$1M+ ARR potential — either greenfield enterprise targets or existing customers with significant expansion opportunity.

🤝 Sales Commitment

FLM, VP, and ABX approval required. AE commits to keeping the account in the program for the full year.

📊 Recent Engagement

Recent sales or marketing interaction confirming account is active and relationship is being worked.

🏆 Strong Account Fit

Strategic alignment with Amplitude's ICP — fintech, media, e-commerce, SaaS, gaming, and enterprise tech.

🎯 Proven ABX Plays

Play Type 1: Account Intelligence Brief ▼

When to use: Before AE meetings with Tier 1 accounts, for executive engagement prep, or when an account enters a key deal stage.

What it includes: Company overview, key stakeholders, recent news, product usage signals, 6sense intent data, open opp context, and personalized talking points.

Who builds it: ABX team member (often Dee for content, Kiersten for coordination). Claude can be used to accelerate research and drafting.

Turnaround: 24–48 hours for standard brief; same-day for urgent exec meetings.

Play Type 2: Targeted Email + Influ2 Ad Sequence ▼

When to use: When a Tier 1 or Tier 2 account shows active 6sense intent signals (Awareness → Consideration stage).

What it includes: 3–4 touchpoint email sequence (via Outreach/Marketo) + matched account-based display ads (via Influ2) targeting the known buying group at the account.

Who activates: ABX team briefs the play; Marketing Ops (Daniel Cuadra) executes in Marketo; Influ2 ads are configured by ABX or digital team.

Duration: Typically 4–6 weeks per account cluster.

Play Type 3: Executive Roundtable / 1:1 Dinner ▼

When to use: For Tier 1 strategic accounts in active pipeline — to accelerate deals, deepen executive relationships, or support expansion conversations.

What it includes: Intimate in-person event (dinner, roundtable, or hosted experience) with 5–15 executive contacts from ABX target accounts, curated by ABX and AE.

Who drives it: ABX owns the guest list and messaging; Field Marketing handles logistics and venue; Sales drives attendance for their accounts.

Lead time: Minimum 6–8 weeks for planning and outreach.

Play Type 4: Intent-Triggered SDR Sequence ▼

When to use: When 6sense detects strong purchase intent signals at a Tier 1 or Tier 2 account without active AE engagement.

What it includes: ABX notifies the relevant SDR and AE with intent context. SDR activates a targeted Outreach sequence using ABX-provided messaging. ABX may support with personalized content or ad layer.

Who coordinates: ABX (signal identification + messaging) + SDR (outreach execution) + AE (strategic overlay).

🎪 Event Process Overview

⬅️ Pre-Event (6–8 weeks out)

  • Finalize guest list with AE and ABX
  • Build ABX account target list for the event
  • Develop invitation messaging and sequence
  • Launch Outreach/Marketo invite sequence
  • Run Influ2 ads targeting registered accounts
  • Brief AEs on attendees before the event

📍 At-Event

  • Capture attendance in Salesforce campaign
  • Flag key account interactions for follow-up
  • AE completes any in-person account briefings
  • Capture action items and next steps in real time

➡️ Post-Event (within 48–72 hours)

  • Upload attendance to Salesforce campaign
  • Trigger post-event follow-up sequence in Marketo
  • Deliver AE follow-up talking points per account
  • Build event recap: attendance, pipeline impact, next steps
  • Share recap with Shab and sales leadership

✍️ Copy & Messaging Standards

Voice & Tone Principles ▼

All ABX content should follow Amplitude's brand voice: Magnetic, Provocative, and Empowering.

🧲 Magnetic

Draw people in. Spark curiosity. Paint a compelling vision. Don't open with "We'd like to invite you." Open with what they'll get out of it.

⚡ Provocative

Challenge assumptions. Be bold and punchy. No hedging or corporate speak. "We help you grow faster" not "We offer solutions that may enhance your operational efficiency."

💪 Empowering

Focus on "you" and what the reader can do. Be the trusted guide, not the authority. Simplify, inspire confidence, make them feel capable.

Writing in one line

Brief. Real. Conversational. Relevant. 8th-grade reading level. Human-to-human, never corporate. Always reference brand.amplitude.com for full guidelines.

Email Subject Line Rules ▼
  • Keep subject lines under 45 characters for mobile rendering
  • Lead with value or curiosity — not a company name or product feature
  • Avoid ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation (!!), and spammy words
  • Personalize with [First Name] or [Company] tokens when appropriate
  • A/B test subject lines on every program — we always have a winner and a variant
  • Preview text should extend the subject line, not repeat it
  • Never use "I wanted to reach out" as a subject or opener — be direct
Personalization Standards ▼

ABX personalization should feel researched, not templated. Avoid lazy personalization like inserting a company name — that's not personalization, it's mail merge.

  • Tier 1 accounts: Full 1:1 personalization — company-specific challenges, recent news, named stakeholder context
  • Tier 2 accounts: Cluster-level personalization — industry/vertical narrative + deal stage messaging
  • Tier 3 accounts: Segment-level personalization — persona-based messaging, intent-signal timing
  • Use Claude for research acceleration — it can find recent company news, 10-K language, exec quotes, and product signals quickly
  • Always sanity-check AI-generated research before using it in outreach
Content Review & Approval Process ▼
  • Email copy: ABX team member drafts → Dee Ward reviews for voice/brand → Shab approves for Tier 1 accounts
  • Event invitations: ABX drafts → Field Marketing reviews logistics → Shab approves messaging
  • Account briefs: ABX team member drafts → AE reviews for accuracy → delivered within 24–48 hours
  • Executive communications: Always routed through Shab before send
  • Anything public-facing: Must go through Brand / PMM review before publication

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between ABX and Account Based Marketing (ABM)? ▼
ABM traditionally refers to targeted marketing campaigns for specific accounts. ABX (Account Based Experience) is a broader motion — it includes marketing, but also sales coordination, customer success alignment, and the entire experience an account has with Amplitude across every touchpoint. ABX is the orchestration layer; ABM is one channel within it.
How do I get an account added to the ABX target list? ▼

Speak to Shab or bring it up in the next Sales Sync. The account team (AE + ABX) reviews the fit criteria: pipeline potential, strategic alignment, and resource capacity. Accounts are formally added via the Account Classification field in Salesforce. The list is reviewed and updated yearly as part of the planning cycle.

 

Do not tell a sales partner that a requested change has been approved, unless it is confirmed by Shab.

How do I know which play to run for a specific account? ▼
Start with the account's signals: What stage is the opportunity? What 6sense buying stage is the account in? Is there an AE requesting support? From there, match the signal to the play type. Intent signal → email + ads sequence. Pre-meeting → account brief. Deal stage stuck → executive engagement. When in doubt, bring it to the ABX team standup — we decide plays together.
How long does it take to build and launch an ABX play? ▼
Depends on the play type. Account brief: 24–48 hours. Email sequence: 1–2 weeks (including Marketo build). Event: 6–8 weeks minimum. Influ2 ad campaign: 1–2 weeks to set up.
What's our relationship with Field Marketing vs. ABX? ▼
Field Marketing runs regional and event programs broadly. ABX provides the account targeting precision for those programs — which accounts to invite, what messaging to use, how to follow up. We collaborate on every event that involves ABX accounts. ABX owns ABX Only events which are typically 3rd party hosted with vendors such as Connex and Ortus Club, but we also run 1:1 and smaller group events.
How do we report on ABX program performance? ▼
Primary reporting happens in Salesforce (campaign membership, pipeline influence) and Tableau (dashboards). For each play or program, ABX team builds a post-program recap covering: accounts engaged, touchpoints delivered, pipeline influenced, deals progressed, and next steps. Recaps go to Shab and relevant AEs. QBR-level summaries go to sales and marketing leadership.
Can I use Claude for account briefs and outreach drafts? ▼
Yes — Claude is a sanctioned tool at Amplitude and encouraged for accelerating research, drafting, and analysis. Use it to pull together company research, summarize news, draft email copy, or build account briefs faster. Always review AI-generated output for accuracy before sending anything externally. Never paste sensitive customer data or deal specifics into any AI tool without confirming with your manager or IT.
What is 6sense and why do we use it? ▼
6sense is our intent data platform. It tracks digital buying signals across the web — when companies are researching topics related to product analytics, digital experience, or analytics platforms — and maps those signals to known accounts. This tells us which of our 118 accounts are "in market" and when. We use 6sense to prioritize which plays to activate and when to time outreach for maximum relevance.
What's the Outreach vs. Marketo split? ▼
Outreach is used by Sales/SDRs for direct 1:1 outbound sequences — rep-sent, personalized emails. Marketo is used for marketing-sent programs — branded campaigns, event invites, nurture sequences at scale. ABX typically provides messaging for both, but we own the Marketo programs and advise on Outreach sequence content. Marketing Ops (Daniel Cuadra) manages Marketo; Sales Ops manages Outreach.

📐 ABX Operating Principles

  • Account first, campaign second. Always start with "What does this account need?" not "What content can we push to them?"
  • AE partnership is non-negotiable. ABX without AE alignment is wasted effort. Every play needs an AE owner who's bought in.
  • If it's not in Asana, it doesn't exist. Log every play, task, and request in Asana before starting.
  • Move fast, document as you go. ABX is fast-moving. Don't let perfect be the enemy of done — but always leave a trail.
  • Data before instinct. Use 6sense, Salesforce, and Amplitude data to validate play selection. Gut feel is a starting point, not a strategy.
  • Recaps are mandatory. Every program gets a post-play recap. If you can't measure it, you can't improve it.
  • Escalate early. If a play is blocked, a stakeholder is unresponsive, or a timeline is slipping — tell Shab immediately. Don't let issues fester.
